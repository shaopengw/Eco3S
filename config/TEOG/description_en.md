# TEOG Simulation – Governance and Livelihood Scenario in the Land of the Two Rivers

## 1. Basic Information

**Experiment Name:** TEOG

**Experiment Objective:** To study the dynamic evolution of population, employment, government governance, and transportation economy in the urban belt of the Two Rivers region, as well as the impact of river navigability on social satisfaction and economic vitality.

**Background:** This simulation uses the cities along the Tigris and Euphrates rivers as its geographic backbone. Residents live, work, and interact within a town network based on real-world coordinates. The government influences livelihoods and river navigability through fiscal and governance means, thereby affecting resident satisfaction and economic performance.

## 2. System Modules
- **Resident System** – Individual agent decision-making
- **Map System** – Town distribution and spatial computation in the Two Rivers region
- **Time System** – Simulation time step management
- **Population System** – Population growth and scale changes
- **Government System** – Policy and public budget management
- **Job Market** – Occupation selection, wage system, and employment allocation
- **Social Network** – Social relationships and information diffusion
- **Transportation Economy** – River navigability and transport costs
- **Climate System** – Impact of climate change on navigability and the economy

## 3. Agent Design

### Resident Agent
- **Quantity:** Initially 10 persons (can grow with birth rate)
- **Key Attributes:**
  - Satisfaction – reflects level of life satisfaction
  - Income – annual income from occupation
  - Job – Farmer / Urban Resident
  - Location – Town where the resident lives
  - Social network connections – social relationships with other residents
- **Core Behaviors (implemented):**
  - **Employment-related:** Seek a job or continue working
  - **Social interaction:** Communicate with neighbors, be influenced by information

### Government Agent
- **Composition:** Multiple OrdinaryGovernmentAgents + HighRankingGovernmentAgent
- **Core Functions:**
  - Budget allocation and policy implementation
  - Investment in river navigability maintenance
  - Social governance and public services
- **Key Attributes:**
  - Budget
  - Tax rate
  - Military strength
- **Decision Mechanism:** Supports group decision-making (2 rounds of discussion when enabled)

## 4. Environment Settings

### 4.1 Geographic Environment
- **Number of towns:** 18 (15 towns along the rivers + 3 other towns)
- **Spatial characteristics:**
  - Two river systems: Tigris and Euphrates
  - Towns distributed along the rivers; river navigability connects economic activities
  - Geographic range: longitude 43.0°–48.0°, latitude 29.0°–36.0°
- **Realism:** Real geographic coordinates (`is_real_world: true`)

### 4.2 Climate Conditions
- **Change pattern:** Reads climate data (`experiment_dataset/climate_data/climate.csv`)
- **Impact pathway:** Climate change affects river navigability, which in turn influences transport costs and economic vitality

### 4.3 Economic Environment
- **Basic occupations and wages:**
  - Farmer: 10 liang/year
  - Urban Resident: 20 liang/year
- **GDP calculation:** Sum of income of all non-government agents

## 5. Key Indicators

**Core indicators monitored during simulation and their significance:**

- **Population** – Reflects changes in social scale
- **Average Satisfaction** – Reflects livelihood and governance effectiveness
- **Government Budget** – Reflects government fiscal capacity
- **Tax Rate** – Affects government revenue and residents' disposable income
- **River Navigability** – Reflects river transport status
- **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)** – Reflects economic vitality
- **Urban Scale** – Reflects the degree of urban agglomeration

## 6. Simulation Parameters

### 6.1 Basic Parameters
- **Initial population:** 10 persons
- **Simulation start year:** 1650
- **Total simulation years:** 2 years
- **Birth rate:** 0.05
- **Map width:** 100
- **Map height:** 150
- **Simulation name:** Not specified (auto‑generated by default)

### 6.2 Behavioral Parameters
- **Response probability:** 0.05
- **Maximum retry attempts:** 3
- **Retry delay:** 1 second
- **Resume from cache:** 0
- **Save cache:** 1 (enabled)

### 6.3 Group Decision Parameters
- **Government group decision:**
  - **Enabled:** 1
  - **Maximum discussion rounds:** 2 rounds

## 7. Interaction Mechanisms

**Key interaction flows among agents:**

- **Resident ↔ Government:**
  - Government policies and budget allocation affect resident satisfaction and employment
  - Tax rate adjustments affect residents' income and overall economic performance

- **Resident ↔ Resident:**
  - Social network spreads information and opinions

- **Resident ↔ Environment:**
  - Changes in river navigability affect employment and economic opportunities
  - Town location influences migration and resource access

## 8. Expected Results

**Research hypotheses:**
1. Improving river navigability will increase economic activity and resident satisfaction
2. Government budget allocation strategies significantly affect social stability and economic performance
3. The structure of the town network influences the spatial diffusion efficiency of resources

**Expected findings:**
- A positive feedback loop exists between river maintenance and economic vitality
- Policy fluctuations cause short‑term satisfaction volatility
- The locational advantages of town nodes lead to uneven development
